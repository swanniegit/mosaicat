Frequently Asked Questions

When are tax returns due in South Africa?
  • Individuals (non-provisional taxpayers): Usually July – October
  • Provisional taxpayers: 1st return: 31 August | 2nd return: End of February | 3rd/top-up (if needed): September
  • Companies: Within 12 months after financial year-end

Exact dates change annually when SARS announces the filing season.

What documents do I need to submit my tax return?
  • IRP5/IT3(a) from your employer
  • Medical aid tax certificate
  • Retirement annuity contribution certificate
  • Travel logbook (if claiming travel expenses)
  • IT3(b) for investment income
  • Proof of other income (rental, freelance, side business)
  • Supporting documents for deductions (donations, home office, etc.)
Do I need to submit supporting documents to SARS?

Not always. In most cases, you don't upload documents immediately — SARS may request verification later. You must keep records for at least 5 years.

What happens if I miss the tax deadline?

You may face:

  • Administrative penalties (monthly fines)
  • Interest on outstanding tax
  • Possible non-compliance status

It's always better to file late than not at all.

Who qualifies as a provisional taxpayer?

You are a provisional taxpayer if you earn income other than a salary, such as:

  • Freelancers / consultants
  • Rental income earners
  • Business owners
  • Commission earners
What is auto-assessment from SARS?

SARS may issue an auto-assessment based on third-party data. If it's correct, accept it. If incorrect, edit and resubmit your return.

Can I claim deductions to reduce my tax?

Yes, depending on your situation. Common deductions include:

  • Retirement annuities
  • Medical expenses (above thresholds)
  • Travel expenses (if applicable)
  • Home office (strict criteria)
  • Donations to approved PBOs
How long does a refund take?

Typically 3–7 working days. Delays can happen if verification is required or banking details need confirmation.

Do I need to submit a tax return if I earn below the threshold?

Not always. You may not need to file if you earn below the annual threshold, have only one employer, and no additional income or deductions. But it's always best to confirm — many people still benefit from submitting.

How can I make tax season easier?
  • Keep documents monthly
  • Use cloud accounting software
  • Separate business and personal finances
  • Work with a registered tax practitioner
